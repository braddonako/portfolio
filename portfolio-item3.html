Rockhounding

This was a team project done by me and two others. We used node.js on the front end, and mongDB and express on the backend. My group partner, Jordan, came up with the idea. I was not aware of what rockhounding was, but Jordan explained it to me. Rockhounding is pretty much like hiking, except you go on excursions looking for rocks that you would like! Currently, there are not many great websites where users can rely on spots to find rocks, so we created a place where users can share where they found the rock, and what kind of rock it is. We preseeded the database, but users are able to go in, choose the rock they found, and tell which state and place where they found it. Since this project was created, I have come a long way with users and their relationships to other data. At this point in my coding journey, I had a hard time authenticating users and making sure that only they could update and delete their data. In my most recent project What to Eat, this problem was solved.
